🌺 Poke Waikiki Staff Checklist
日本語 English

This checklist was created to remind everyone that what matters most is working with awareness every time. Of course, people may forget things or make mistakes. However, if each person stays mindful every day when starting work, these actions will naturally become habits. Please stay aware and act with intention.

Note: This is not about thinking, “That person is not doing it, but I am,” or comparing yourself to others. That is not what this checklist is for, so please do not misunderstand its purpose. Let’s help each other, stay mindful every day, and work together so that we can keep doing these things consistently every time.

  1. Slogan: “Serve hot food while it is hot, and cold food while it is cold.” Example: During idle time around 3 or 4 PM, there are no customers, so the chicken at the front may have been sitting out for over 40 minutes. You might think, “It’s a little cold and hard, but it’s probably okay,” and serve it anyway. Please think about that. If the customer waits 5 more minutes, they could receive an extremely fresh and delicious poke bowl. If there is a backup prepared 20 minutes earlier in the back, serving that may still taste good, but compared with our very best poke bowl, the quality drops a lot. Please stay aware of the business situation and make sure each person can serve delicious poke bowls. Let’s raise our awareness of serving tasty food quickly and carefully.
  2. Do not lie. Do not lie about your work. Do not slack off. Always look for something to do, and if you do not know what to do, ask the person in charge. Looking at your phone because it is slow, or talking for a long time, is strictly prohibited.
  3. Do not take company property from the store. This should be obvious, but please separate company property from your own belongings. If someone takes something home without permission, regardless of whether it is big or small, we will first contact the police and treat it as theft. There will be no exceptions. Wrong is wrong.
  4. Cell phones may only be used in emergencies, and only after informing the person in charge.
  5. Always tell someone before going to the restroom. Also, if restroom use frequently takes 15, 20, or 30 minutes, inappropriate use may become subject to warning. Use the restroom on the 2nd floor. If it is crowded, use the 1st or 4th floor. Please remove your uniform before using the restroom. When you return to the store, washing your hands again is mandatory.
  6. If a customer is looking at the sign or menu board, speak to them immediately. Even when you are busy working in the kitchen, always stay aware of the front. If someone is looking at the menu, go to the front right away, say hello, and explain things such as the bowl sizes. Important: Do not make your own decision that “they’re just looking, so it’s fine.” That is not okay. Go to the front first. Having a staff member at the front at all times is basic restaurant common sense. Normally, having nobody there is not acceptable. With only two staff members, there will often be work inside, but please respond flexibly. Stay aware of the front.
  7. Follow the drink rules. The company provides one bottle of water per person, or two bottles if you work both lunch and dinner. However, please do not drink the large water bottles. Other juices and drinks must be purchased at the staff price.
  8. Do not be late. It is basic common sense that by the time your shift starts, you should already be in uniform, have washed your hands, and clocked in. Regularly arriving late makes it difficult to work here and causes people to lose trust in you. Please be on time. If you are going to be late, contact us.
  9. Staff meal is one bowl per person, and the portion should be the same as a regular bowl. If someone is taking more than that, please report it. If something was made by mistake, remove only the protein properly in the kitchen and use it later if possible. It is best if it can be eaten as staff meal, but please ask first.

For our food court business, one of the most important things is to keep the inside of the front refrigerator beautiful and clean. It is the heart of the store. If the front looks dirty, customers will feel less interested in buying.

The front glass must always be wiped in the morning and evening, and the vegetables and fish should always look full and delicious. Please also keep the inside of the refrigerator clean at all times.

★ Customer first! If we do not sell, we do not make profit. That is the basic principle. Please communicate with each other and work together so operations move smoothly.

★ Food poisoning! Because we handle raw products, please be thorough with handwashing, glove changes, and sanitation control. If food poisoning happens, the store and the company could both be finished.

Please check the items below. We will hand this out every month, so please review it carefully. There will be things you can do, things you are not doing yet, and things you did not know. Let’s make sure we understand them clearly.
